Untangle – Privacy Policy

Last updated:

Overview

Untangle helps you understand your screen habits through clear, on‑device analytics. All processing happens locally on your computer. Untangle does not send data to any server or third party.

What information we process (locally)

Untangle computes and stores only the minimum needed to show time and insights:

  • Domain‑level time and daily aggregates: seconds per domain, minute‑level activity buckets, number of tab switches, tab churn, deep/micro sessions, longest focus streak, and derived daily features (e.g., productive %, switches/hr, day vs. night minutes).
  • Daily labels and suggestions: an interpretable label (e.g., “Focused”, “Fragmented”) with confidence, plus optional “things to look out for”.
  • Your settings and overrides: site categories you enter and whether you mark sites as Productive/Unproductive/Misc.
  • On‑device model: when enough days exist, Untangle stores a small, local clustering model (centroids + standardization stats + cluster→label mapping) to personalize labels. No data leaves your device.
  • Runtime snapshot (session storage): ephemeral state (e.g., last tick time) to make tracking robust across service worker restarts.

What we do not process

  • No personally identifiable information (PII), no emails/messages, no health or financial data.
  • No full page content, no keystrokes, no mouse clicks, no scroll logs.
  • No web history list (Untangle doesn’t use the History API). We only extract the hostname of the active tab (e.g., docs.google.com) for time attribution; paths/parameters are not stored.
  • No IP, GPS, or device location.

How we use the information

  • To compute and display domain‑level time, daily labels, focus metrics, and trends in the popup and Options pages.
  • To personalize labels on‑device after you’ve accumulated enough days (still mapped back to clear, interpretable categories).
  • To export your data to CSV/JSON on request and to respect your “Delete all data” action.

What we share

Nothing. Untangle does not transmit or share your data with any external server, third party, or analytics platform.

Permissions and why we request them

  • tabs: Observe active tab changes and attribute time to domains. We do not read or modify page content.
  • windows: Know when Chrome is focused so time only counts during active use.
  • idle: Respect away state and apply a short “reading” grace so passive reading counts appropriately.
  • alarms: A lightweight 1‑minute pulse that keeps time accurate while reading (when no other events fire).
  • storage: Store settings, daily aggregates, and the on‑device model (local & session storage).
  • webNavigation (optional): If you enable it in Options, we detect SPA route changes to improve tab‑churn accuracy. No content is inspected.

Optional HTTPS site access (origins)

At first use, the popup requests optional host access for https://*/*. This one‑time consent lets Untangle read the active tab’s URL so it can extract the hostname only (e.g., example.com) for time attribution. No page content or paths/parameters are stored.

  • You can accept to enable per‑site time attribution across HTTPS sites.
  • You can decline; if declined, Untangle cannot attribute time to sites and core features may not function.
  • You can revoke access anytime at chrome://extensions → Untangle → Site access.

Security

  • All data is kept in Chrome’s local storage on your device.
  • No external transmission; no remote servers or third‑party SDKs receive your data.
  • Standard OS and browser protections apply to local files on your device.

Data retention, access, and deletion

  • Retention: Data remains on your device until you delete it or uninstall the extension.
  • Access: You can view your summaries and export JSON/CSV in Options.
  • Deletion: Use “Delete all data” in Options to remove all stored data. Uninstalling the extension also removes its stored data.

User choices and controls

  • Enable/disable tracking in Options or the popup switch.
  • Grant/deny HTTPS site access from the popup and revoke later in chrome://extensions.
  • Enable/disable SPA route tracking (webNavigation) in Options.
  • Edit categories and productivity flags to shape insights; mark sites as Productive/Unproductive/Misc.

Children’s privacy

Untangle is a general‑audience tool and does not knowingly collect personal information from children. Because Untangle does not collect or transmit personal data, no server‑side child data processing occurs.

Changes to this policy
